Late Consoles

Late Nintendo Consoles

Wii, Wii U, Switch

2004 - present

The late Nintendo Consoles contain the Wii, Wii U, and Switch. This era began in 2006 with the release of the Wii. Between these three consoles, Nintendo have sold a combined total of over 237 million units.

Wii

Wii Would Like to Play

The Wii is a console Nintendo developed to target a wide demographic of players through "novel gameplay." It is the seventh best-selling console of all time.

The Wii Remote is a wireless controller that has motion sensing capability in addition to traditional controls. This allows it to be used as a pointer towards a display, or to recognize gestures the user performs.

The Wii was the first Nintendo console with internet capability. It is also capable of playing Gamecube games and connecting Gamecub controllers.

Wii U

How U Will Play Next

The Wii U was developed to be the successor to the Wii. It however was nowhere near as successful was the Wii, and is Nintendo's worst selling home console.

The Wii U is the first Nintendo console to support HD graphics. The primary controller is the Wii U Gamepad, which contains a touchscreen in addition to standard controller buttons.

The Wii U also supports standard Wii controllers, and the Wii U Pro Controller, which is closer to a standard game controller. It is backwards compatible with Wii games.

Switch

A Switch in Gaming

The Nintendo Swtich is a hybrid console, allowing for use in handheld mode and in docked mode. It is Nintendo's best selling home console, and the third best selling game console of all time.

The console is a touchscren tablet with controlelrs known as joycons attached. The Joycons feature standard control layout as well as motion detection for gesture controllers. They are removeable from the Switch itself.

The goal with the Swith was to build on the Wii U's idea of a tablet gamepad into smomething bigger and better, which they were clearly able to achieve as seen through their sales stats.
